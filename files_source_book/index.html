Sandhyā Vandhanam
Āśvalāyana Trikāla Sandhyā — Complete Translation in Two Parts

Sandhyā Vandhanam (also titled Āśvalāyana Trikāla Sandhyā) is a compendium of Vedic daily rituals compiled for the Āśvalāyana school of the Ṛgveda, curated by Shri Sundarshastri of Tejavar. It covers 18 chapters of rites performed three times daily: at dawn, noon, and dusk.

This translation covers both parts of the original scanned PDF: Part I contains the Marathi preface and 18-chapter table of contents; Part II contains the complete Sanskrit ritual text from pages 5–13 of the original, including all mantras, procedures, and word-by-word breakdowns accessible through dropdown menus below each section.

Book Summary

Sandhyā Vandhanam is a practical self-study guide to the Vedic daily rituals, compiled for the Āśvalāyana school of the Ṛgveda. Its Marathi preface addresses a social reality of the time: Sanskrit learning had become restricted to professional priests, leaving householders unable to perform even basic daily rites without external help. The book aims to restore that independence while emphasising that priests still deserve respect, dakshinā, and proper honour for major rites.

The ritual text (Part II) begins with auspicious invocations and seven Mahābhārata verses — the essence Vyāsa taught his son Śuka — which establish the eternal nature of dharma over worldly pleasures and fears. The ritual sequence then follows in careful order:

Bhasmadhāraṇa — applying sacred ash to eight body points with five Śaiva mantras from the Rudrāṣṭādhyāyī, plus the Vāmadeva eight-name sequence. The ash is understood as the final truth of all five elements. The tripuṇḍra (three ash lines) may be applied with OM Namaḥ Śivāya, the Gāyatrī, or the Praṇava.

Yajñopavīta Dhāraṇa — wearing the new sacred thread consecrated ten times with Gāyatrī, and releasing the old thread with the formula "go to the sea — svāhā."

Āśvalāyana Sandhyāprayoga — the Sandhyā ritual proper begins with Ācamana (sipping water while touching 24 body points with 24 names of Viṣṇu, a form of nyāsa), then Prāṇāyāma (breath-regulation with the Gāyatrī and seven cosmic-plane utterances), then the formal Saṅkalpa (vow to perform the rite for the Supreme Lord). The purification sequence follows: first Mārjana (sprinkling oneself with the Āpo Hiṣṭhā hymn, Ṛgveda 10.9), Mantracamana (sipping water with the Sūrya-Agni wrath-purification mantra), second Mārjana (nine-verse form), and Aghamarṣaṇa (the powerful rite where the sin-person is expelled through the right nostril while reciting the cosmic-origin hymn of Cosmic Order, truth, ocean, year, and Sun-Moon). Finally, Arghyadāna (three offerings of water to the Sun with the Gāyatrī), Āsana (the earth-seat mantra purifying the seat for worship), and the full Gāyatrī invocation summoning the divine powers of Gāyatrī, Sāvitrī, Sarasvatī, and all the Chandas seers.
